On my first backpacking trip, I lost sight of camp at dusk.
I didn't find my tent until 5am the following morning.
In the interceding hours, the weather was ominous.
Humid, wet, thunderous, clear and 40 degree drop no less.
A lot of things went through my mind during that period.
At one moment I recall a break from exhaustion. Curled up in a ball, drenched, and sore
I found solace in the milky way and shooting stars in the universe.
In spite of wet socks, sore limbs, and exhaustion, I got up, put my head lamp on trekked back to camp.
I have been walking ever since.
